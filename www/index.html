slime mould oscillation model

This is a model of a tubule in the slime mould Physarum Polycephalum as a series of coupled oscillators. As one segment of the tubule contracts, it pushes volume into neighbouring regions, affecting that area's ability to contract and expand. We believe that this might be a possible model for how information is transferred across a slime mould, and a mechanism for the 'peristaltic wave' observed in real Physarum samples. Shown here is an interactive interface that allows the parameters of the simulation to be manipulated, demonstrating their effects on a real slime. Right now, this simulation is quite basic, however more complexity will be added over the coming months.
