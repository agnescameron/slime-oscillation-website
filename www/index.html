slime mould oscillation model

The slime mold, Physarum polycephalum, is a single-celled organism that can make decisions. Although it doesn't possess a nervous system, it can sense multiple stimuli, compare their qualities, and "choose" or "reject" food sources. This interactive website treats a tubule (a single 'branch') of the slime mould as a set of connected "oscillators". Each oscillator has an intrinsic pattern of pulsations, and can communicate with its neighbors. The cell (or this set of oscillators) collectively makes a decision to either choose or reject a food source.

You can play with the parameters that affect the strength of coupling, the intrinsic oscillation characteristics or the amplitude of oscillations. Now your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to tweak the parameters and help the slime mold make a decision!
